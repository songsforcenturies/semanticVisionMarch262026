## 1. PLATFORM OVERVIEW AND VISION

### What Is Semantic Vision?

Semantic Vision is the world's first AI-powered personalized educational narrative platform. Instead of giving every child the same book, Semantic Vision generates unique, personalized stories for each child -- stories that:

- **Reflect their faith** (Christianity, Islam, Buddhism, Hinduism, Judaism, Secular Humanism, and more)
- **Honor their cultural heritage** (African American, South Asian, Latin American, East Asian, and more)
- **Teach vocabulary at their exact level** using a scientifically-designed 60/30/10 three-tier distribution
- **Celebrate their strengths** and gently model growth in their areas for improvement
- **Feature real brands as problem-solving heroes** within the narrative (not as ads, but as organic story elements)
- **Measure actual comprehension** of every element, including brand content, through AI-generated questions

### The Six Stakeholder Roles

| Role | What They Do | How They Access |
|---|---|---|
| Parent/Guardian | Manage children, set preferences, monitor progress, control brand exposure, manage wallet | Email + Password |
| Student | Read personalized stories, take assessments, record themselves reading, compete in spelling bees | Student Code + PIN |
| Teacher | Create classroom sessions, monitor students in real-time, review performance | Email + Password |
| Brand Partner | Create campaigns, fund story integrations, review Brand Comprehension analytics | Email + Password |
| Affiliate | Refer new users, earn commissions, track performance | Via Guardian Portal |
| Administrator | Manage everything: users, plans, word banks, contests, messaging, system config | Email + Password |

### How Stories Are Generated

1. Parent creates a student profile with all personalization dimensions
2. Student or parent requests a new story
3. The system assembles a composite AI prompt from 10+ dimensions
4. A Large Language Model generates a unique, 5-chapter educational narrative
5. Vocabulary is distributed at 60% reinforcement, 30% growth, 10% aspiration
6. If parent has opted in, brand products appear as organic problem-solving elements
7. Comprehension questions are generated per chapter
8. The story is delivered to the student's personal library

### 2.3 Parental Controls and Recording Enforcement

**This is a patent-pending feature unique to Semantic Vision.**

Found under the "Reading Rules" dropdown on each student's card.

**Recording Requirement:**
Choose from four modes:
- **Optional** (default): Your child can choose to record themselves reading, but it's never required
- **Audio Required:** Your child MUST record themselves reading aloud before they can advance to the next chapter
- **Video Required:** Your child MUST record video of themselves reading
- **Both Required:** Both audio and video recordings are mandatory

**Chapter Threshold:**
Set the chapter number at which recording becomes mandatory.
- 0 = Required for EVERY chapter
- 1 = Required starting from Chapter 1 (every chapter)
- 3 = Your child reads Chapters 1-2 freely, then must record from Chapter 3 onward
- This lets younger readers warm up before the accountability kicks in

**Auto-Start Recording:**
When enabled, the recording interface opens automatically when your child reaches a chapter that requires recording. They don't have to remember to click "Record" -- it's right there.

**Require Confirmation:**
When enabled, your child must explicitly confirm "Yes, I want to proceed" before passing a recording checkpoint. This adds an extra layer of accountability.

**Can Skip Recording (Hidden Setting):**
- When OFF: The "Finish Chapter" button is physically DISABLED until the recording is submitted. Your child literally cannot advance without recording. A warning banner reads: "Complete the recording above before you can finish this chapter."
- When ON: Your child sees the recording prompt but can dismiss it

**Why This Matters:**
- You can verify your child actually read the book (not just clicked through)
- Recordings are preserved forever in your Audio Memories library
- Diction scores show pronunciation accuracy, fluency, completeness, and prosody
- You can track improvement over months and years

### 2.13 PARENT FAQ (25 Questions)

**Q1: How many stories can my child read?**
A: Unlimited. Every story is uniquely generated by AI. Your child will never receive the same story twice.

**Q2: Are the stories appropriate for my child's age?**
A: Yes. The 16-level age-calibration system (Claim 16) ensures sentence length, vocabulary complexity, and narrative themes match your child's developmental stage. A 5-year-old gets 5-8 word sentences with sight words; a 15-year-old gets SAT-prep vocabulary with sophisticated themes.

**Q3: How does the AI know what to write about?**
A: The AI receives a composite prompt incorporating your child's name, age, grade, interests, virtues, emotions, belief system, cultural context, strengths, weaknesses, vocabulary banks, and language preference -- all at once. This is why every story is truly personalized.

**Q4: Will my child see advertisements?**
A: NEVER in the traditional sense. There are no banner ads, pop-ups, or video interstitials. If you opt in to brand content, brand products appear as natural, problem-solving elements within the story. Default is OFF.

**Q5: What is "Brand Comprehension"?**
A: It's our patented metric (Claim 2) that measures whether your child actually understood the brand's role in the story. For brands, an 85% Brand Comprehension score means 85% of students understood how the product helped solve the character's problem. This is fundamentally different from a meaningless click-through rate.

**Q6: Can I see what my child is reading?**
A: Yes. You can view all generated stories in the Progress tab, listen to their recordings in Audio Memories, and review their comprehension scores.

**Q7: What if I want my child to prove they actually read?**
A: Use Parental Controls (Reading Rules). Set recording to "Audio Required" and "Can Skip" to OFF. Your child physically cannot advance past a chapter without recording themselves reading it aloud.

**Q8: What happens to the recordings?**
A: They are preserved forever in your Audio Memories library. You can play them back, download them, and track diction improvement over time. Imagine listening to your child reading at age 8, then again at 12, at 16...

**Q9: Can my child read in our native language?**
A: Yes. Select the language during student creation. The entire story, including vocabulary explanations and comprehension questions, is generated in that language. 20+ languages are supported.

**Q10: How does the referral program work?**
A: Share your referral code (visible in the Invite & Earn tab). When someone registers with your code, both of you receive $5.00 wallet credit. Active contests offer additional prizes.

**Q11: What is the Agentic Reach Score?**
A: It's our holistic learning metric (Claim 15). It combines vocabulary mastery and completed narratives into a single score (0-100). It measures how much your child is growing overall, not just on individual tests.

**Q12: What is the Biological Vocabulary Target?**
A: It's the scientifically-derived expected vocabulary for your child's age (Claim 47). For example, an 8-year-old is expected to know approximately 4,000 words. Semantic Vision shows your child's progress toward this target.

**Q13: Can I create my own word banks?**
A: Yes. Click "Create Word Bank" in the Marketplace tab. Add your own words with definitions and example sentences. Assign them to your child's profile.

**Q14: How do subscriptions work?**
A: Free accounts get 2 students with basic access. Paid plans ($3.99-$19.99/mo) offer more students, priority generation, and marketplace access. Enter coupon or invitation codes for discounts.

**Q15: Can my child use this offline?**
A: Yes. Semantic Vision is a Progressive Web App (Claim 69). Install it on your home screen, save stories for offline reading, and sync when you're back online.

**Q16: What is the Spelling Bee feature?**
A: Administrators create timed spelling contests (Claim 10). Your child can compete against other students, with words drawn from their vocabulary banks. Leaderboards rank by accuracy and speed.

**Q17: Will my child receive task reminders?**
A: Yes. The Task Reminder system (Claim 96) automatically detects unfinished stories, upcoming spelling contests, and unread messages, and shows them on your child's dashboard.

**Q18: Can different children have different rules?**
A: Yes. Parental controls are per-student (Claim 58). You can have strict recording requirements for your younger child and optional recording for your older one.

**Q19: How is my family's data protected?**
A: Students log in with code/PIN (not email), brand consent defaults to false, analytics are anonymized, and all data is stored securely.

**Q20: Can I see how my child compares to their age group?**
A: The Biological Vocabulary Target and Agentic Reach Score provide age-normalized benchmarks. Spelling Bee leaderboards show competitive ranking.

**Q21: What are virtues and why do they matter?**
A: Virtues are character traits (Patience, Honesty, Courage, etc.) that the AI models through the story protagonist's behavior. When your child reads about a character demonstrating Patience, they internalize that virtue through narrative engagement.

**Q22: What are emotional intelligence categories?**
A: These are emotional themes (Joy, Sadness, Anger, Fear, Hope, etc.) that the AI weaves into character development. If you select "Anger" as a category, the AI will create characters who model healthy anger management.

**Q23: How do I get notified about platform updates?**
A: The Notification Bell in the header shows unread admin messages. Administrators send targeted messages for announcements, updates, and important information.

**Q24: Can my child share recordings with the community?**
A: Yes, if you opt in. Recordings can be contributed to the Peer Audio Book Section where other children listen to peer narrations.

**Q25: What makes this different from other educational apps?**
A: No other platform simultaneously personalizes content across faith, culture, virtues, emotions, strengths, weaknesses, interests, vocabulary level, and age -- while integrating brands as organic story elements and measuring comprehension. This combination is entirely novel and patent-pending.

### 3.6 Read Aloud Recording and Diction Analysis

**How It Works:**
1. Click "Read This Chapter Aloud" at the top of the chapter
2. Read the story text out loud, clearly and at a comfortable pace
3. Click "Stop Recording" when finished
4. Wait a moment while the AI analyzes your reading
5. See your scores!

**Your Scores:**
- **Pronunciation Accuracy (0-100):** Did you pronounce words correctly?
- **Fluency (0-100):** Did you read smoothly without too many pauses?
- **Completeness (0-100):** Did you read all the words?
- **Prosody (0-100):** Did you read with expression and feeling?

**Getting Better:**
Every time you record, the system tracks your improvement. Over weeks and months, you'll see your scores go up. The AI even creates stories with words you've been mispronouncing, giving you practice!

### 3.7 Comprehension Questions

After each chapter, you answer a comprehension question:
- 4 multiple-choice options
- The question tests whether you understood what happened in the chapter
- Get it right and you move on!
- These questions may include questions about brand products in the story (if your parent opted in)

### 3.8 Vocabulary Assessment and Mastery

After completing all 5 chapters, you may be asked to demonstrate mastery of the target and stretch vocabulary words:
- The AI asks you to define words and use them in sentences
- You're scored on: definition accuracy, contextual usage, and spelling
- Score 80% or higher on a word and it's marked as "mastered"
- Mastered words are removed from future stories so you always learn NEW words

5. The contest auto-submits when time runs out

**Leaderboard:**
After the contest, see how you rank against other students! Ranking is by:

### 5.3 How Brand Story Integration Works (The Revolution)

**This is NOT advertising. This is Brand Comprehension.**

Traditional digital advertising: a banner ad appears next to educational content. A child ignores it. The brand gets a "0.3% click-through rate."

Semantic Vision's approach: a child reads a story where the protagonist faces a real problem. The character discovers a real product (YOUR product) that helps solve that problem. The child reads about how the product works within the narrative. Then the child is asked a comprehension question about the story, which may include the brand element.

**Example:**
"SJ was building a race car for the big championship but couldn't find the right tools. Then he discovered [Your Product], which had exactly the precision instruments he needed. Using the [Product Feature], SJ carefully assembled the engine..."

The child then answers: "What helped SJ solve his engineering challenge?" -- and 85% of students answer correctly. That's an 85% Brand Comprehension Score.

### 5.4 Brand Comprehension: The Metric That Changes Everything

**What It Is:**
Brand Comprehension (Patent Claims 2, 9) measures the percentage of students who correctly answered comprehension questions related to your brand's presence in the story.

**How It's Calculated:**
```
Brand Comprehension Score = (correct answers on brand questions / total attempts on brand questions) x 100
```

**The Four-Condition Question Classification (Claim 2):**
The system identifies which comprehension questions relate to your brand using four conditions:
- **Condition A:** Question text or answers contain your brand/product name
- **Condition B:** Product category keyword + question from brand-containing chapter
- **Condition C:** Answer options contain derivatives of your product names
- **Condition D:** Question keywords appear near brand mentions in the chapter

**Why This Changes Everything:**
- An 85% Brand Comprehension Score means 85% of children UNDERSTOOD your product's role in solving a character's problem
- Compare this to a 0.3% banner ad click-through rate
- This is the first time in history that cognitive engagement with brand content in education can be measured

### 5.6 The Competitive Bidding Marketplace
